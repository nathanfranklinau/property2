If you want to subdivide your property in Queensland, you will need to lodge a **Reconfiguration of a Lot (ROL)** application with your local council. This is the formal name for a subdivision development application under the *Planning Act 2016*.

Here is what you need to know.

## What Is an ROL?

A Reconfiguration of a Lot is any change to the configuration of land, including:

- **Subdividing** one lot into two or more lots
- **Amalgamating** two or more lots into one
- **Rearranging boundaries** between existing lots
- **Creating easements** over land
- **Creating common property** (for community title schemes)

The most common ROL is a straightforward residential subdivision — splitting one house block into two lots.

## When Do You Need an ROL?

You need an ROL application any time you want to change the legal boundaries of a property. This includes:

- Splitting a large lot into two or more smaller lots
- Adjusting the boundary between your lot and a neighbour's lot
- Creating a new access easement
- Converting to community or group title

You do **not** need an ROL to:
- Build a granny flat or secondary dwelling on your existing lot (this is a building application, not an ROL)
- Fence your property
- Lease part of your property

## Code Assessment vs Impact Assessment

Your ROL application will be assessed under one of two pathways:

### Code Assessment

- Faster and simpler
- 20 business days for council to decide
- No public notification required
- Used when the proposal complies with the planning scheme's acceptable outcomes
- Most straightforward residential subdivisions fall into this category

### Impact Assessment

- Slower and more complex
- Includes a public notification period (minimum 15 business days)
- Third parties can make submissions (and potentially appeal)
- Used when the proposal does not fully comply with acceptable outcomes, or in zones where subdivision requires impact assessment
- More expensive due to longer timeframes and additional reporting

Your town planner can advise which pathway applies to your property.

## The ROL Process Step by Step

### 1. Prepare your application

You will need:
- A completed application form (lodged via PD Hub)
- A site plan showing proposed lot boundaries and dimensions
- A town planning report or statement of proposal
- Supporting technical reports (stormwater, services, traffic — if required)

### 2. Lodge via PD Hub

The QLD Government's online planning portal is the standard lodgement point. You pay the application fee at this stage.

### 3. Council acknowledges receipt

Council issues an **Acknowledgement Notice** and begins their assessment. They may issue an **Information Request** if they need more details.

### 4. Receive the Decision Notice

Council approves (with conditions) or refuses. Common conditions include:
- Infrastructure charges to be paid
- Civil works to be completed (stormwater, access, services)
- Easements to be created or surrendered
- Landscaping or fencing requirements

### 5. Comply with conditions

Before you can register new titles, all conditions must be satisfied:
- Pay infrastructure charges
- Complete required works
- Provide evidence of compliance to council

### 6. Survey plan sealing and title registration

Your surveyor prepares the final Survey Plan. Council seals it after confirming compliance. The plan is then lodged with Titles Queensland, and new titles are issued.

## What Does an ROL Cost?

| Item | Typical Range |
|---|---|
| Council application fee | $500 - $5,000 |
| Town planner fees | $2,000 - $8,000 |
| Surveyor fees | $3,000 - $8,000 |
| Infrastructure charges | $20,000 - $60,000+ |
| Civil works | $10,000 - $50,000+ |
| Utility connections | $5,000 - $20,000 |

Infrastructure charges are typically the largest single cost and vary significantly between councils.

## Common Reasons for Refusal

ROL applications can be refused if:

- The proposed lots do not meet minimum size requirements
- Inadequate road frontage or access
- The proposal conflicts with an overlay (flood, heritage, character)
- Insufficient infrastructure capacity
- The application does not demonstrate compliance with the planning scheme

## Tips for a Successful ROL

1. **Check eligibility first** — confirm lot size, zoning, and frontage before spending money on applications
2. **Get a pre-lodgement meeting** — council feedback early saves time and money
3. **Engage a town planner** — they know the planning scheme inside out and can navigate council processes
4. **Engage a surveyor early** — they can identify boundary issues and easements before you commit
5. **Budget conservatively** — infrastructure charges and civil works often exceed initial estimates

## Key Takeaway

An ROL is the legal gateway to subdivision in Queensland. Understanding the process, costs, and assessment pathway before you start helps you make informed decisions and avoid expensive surprises.
