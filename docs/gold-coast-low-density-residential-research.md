# Gold Coast Low Density Residential Zone — Research Findings

> **Researched against authoritative Gold Coast City Council sources only.**
> Last updated from: Gold Coast City Plan **Version 13** (commenced 9 December 2025).
> Zone code substance last changed by OCOP Major Update 2 & 3 (City Plan Version 10, 4 July 2023).
> Versions 11–13 are minor/administrative amendments only and do not alter zone purpose statements.

---

## 1. Definition — Zone Purpose

**Section 6.2.1.2, Part 6.2.1 — Low Density Residential Zone Code**

> **(1)** The purpose of the Low density residential zone code is to provide for **Dwelling houses**, supported by community uses and small-scale services and facilities that cater for local residents' daily needs.

---

## 2. Overall Outcomes — How the Purpose Is Achieved

**Section 6.2.1.2(2)**

The purpose of the code is achieved through the following overall outcomes:

### (a) Land Uses

| # | Outcome |
|---|---------|
| (i) | Land uses consist of a range of **low intensity, low rise, predominantly detached housing** that retains and enhances local character and amenity by maintaining existing scale, building height and intensity despite proximity to public transport or other services. |
| (ii) | Uses include **Dwelling houses, Community residences, Rooming accommodation** (housing no more than four unrelated persons) and **Home based businesses**; which maintain a high level of residential amenity and protect the local character of the zone. |
| (iii) | Uses such as **Dual occupancies and Multiple dwellings** occur in low concentrations where they achieve a dispersed or gentle-scattering effect. They are found on: (A) lots with dual frontage; or (B) lots identified on the Residential density overlay map with a designated residential density of RD1 or greater. |
| (iv) | **Residential care facilities and Retirement facilities** are only found on lots within a 400 m walking distance of high frequency public transport stops, mixed use centres, neighbourhood centres, or district or regional level community facilities. |
| (v) | **Neighbourhood centres and stand-alone small scale non-residential development** are only present where consistent with the Strategic framework. |
| (vi) | **Incompatible uses** — Party houses, Service stations, Short-term accommodation, Hotels, medium-to-large-scale Places of worship, and Tourist attractions — are not located in the zone. |
| (vii) | Uses do not detract from the residential amenity of the area. |

### (b) Amenity

Amenity reasonably expected in a low density, permanent residential environment is achieved, having regard to:
- (A) the qualities of the locality;
- (B) built form and scale;
- (C) noise, privacy, safety, glare, odour and light; and
- (D) traffic, parking, servicing and hours of operation.

### (c) Built Form and Scale

| # | Outcome |
|---|---------|
| (i) | Building and structure height does not exceed the height shown on the **Building height overlay map**. |
| (ii) | Does not exceed a **Partial third storey** where the Building height overlay map indicates a building height of 9 metres. |
| (iii) | Is responsive to site characteristics; maximises opportunities for landscaping, encourages outdoor living, and provides adequate space for services and utilities. |
| (iv) | Is setback from side and rear boundaries to protect the privacy and amenity of adjoining residences. |
| (v) | Is setback from road frontages to complement the streetscape character and provide space for off-street parking. |
| (vi) | Maintains a site cover consistent with the planned character; does not present an appearance of bulk; maximises opportunities for landscaping and outdoor living. |

### (d) Neighbourhood Character

- Low intensity, locally serviced suburban neighbourhoods offering a high level of amenity and contributing to a sense of openness, with buildings that present well to the street and are set amongst generous landscaping.
- Streets characterised by street trees and shared use, with a network of interconnecting thoroughfares for pedestrians, cyclists and slow-moving vehicles.
- Freestanding garages and carports designed to positively contribute to the surrounding streetscape.

### (e) Lot Design

- Practical shaped sites for intended development.
- Space for on-street parking and street trees.
- Supports traditional suburban residential living, or an opportunity for lots as small as 400 m² (but no less) where they front dual frontages or are identified on the Minimum lot size overlay map.

## 3. Precinct Variations

Three precincts impose **additional** overall outcomes within the Low Density Residential zone:

### Large Lot Precinct
- Identifies and protects larger lots with particular constraints or local character and amenity values.
- Land uses are limited to **Dwelling houses only**.
- Subdivision of land is not envisaged.
- Residential density: limited to one dwelling house per lot (no acceptable outcome provided for variation).
- New lots cannot be created within this precinct.

### Calypso Bay Precinct
- Cumulative residential density (across this precinct and the Calypso Bay precinct in the Medium density residential zone) does not exceed **1,852 dwellings**.
- Development does not compromise planned infrastructure delivery or the achievement of other parts of the precinct.

### Sovereign Island Precinct
- Land uses are limited to **Dwelling houses or Home based business** only. No acceptable outcome provided — all development must directly satisfy the performance outcome.

## 4. Specific Benchmarks

### Accepted Development (Part A — Table 6.2.1-1)

| Benchmark | Standard |
|-----------|----------|
| **Front setback** | 6 m (standard); 4.5 m to wall/balcony for small lots |
| **Side/rear setback** | 1.5 m (up to 4.5 m height); 2 m (4.5–7.5 m); +0.5 m per additional 3 m above 7.5 m |
| **Site cover** | Maximum 50% |
| **Building height** | Maximum 2 storeys / 9 m |
| **Freestanding garages/carports** | Maximum 3.5 m height |
| **Residential density** | Limited to one dwelling house; or per Residential density overlay map; or max 1 dwelling per 400 m² where not shown on overlay map |

### Assessable Development (Part B — Table 6.2.1-2)

Key performance outcomes:

| Performance Outcome | Standard |
|--------------------|----------|
| **PO1 — Non-residential uses** | Small scale and stand-alone (single tenancy; not adjoining another non-residential use) |
| **PO3 — Setbacks** | As per accepted development table above |
| **PO4 — Site cover** | Maximum 50% |
| **PO5 — Building height** | Low rise; max 2 storeys / 9 m (or partial third storey within 9 m) |
| **PO6 — Garages/carports** | Very low rise; max 3.5 m |
| **PO7 — Density** | Low density to complement existing neighbourhood; protect Dwelling house character. Max 1 dwelling per 400 m² where not on overlay map |
| **PO10 — Minimum lot size** | 600 m² (standard); 400 m² for dual frontage lots or lots on Minimum lot size overlay map |
| **PO11 — Lot configuration** | Min 17 m road frontage (4.5 m for rear lots) |

## 5. Key Characteristics Summary

The Low Density Residential zone in the Gold Coast LGA is defined by:

- **Primary use:** Detached single dwellings (Dwelling houses)
- **Scale:** Low-rise (maximum 2 storeys / 9 m)
- **Density:** Low — effectively one dwelling per lot (or capped by overlay map)
- **Character:** Suburban, detached, green, open, street-oriented with generous landscaping
- **Intensification:** Strictly limited — Dual occupancies and Multiple dwellings allowed only on specific lots (dual frontage or Residential density overlay map RD1+)
- **Non-residential uses:** Tiny scale only — neighbourhood shops or community services consistent with the Strategic framework
- **Incompatible uses explicitly excluded:** Party houses, Service stations, Short-term accommodation (e.g. Airbnb-type), Hotels, large Places of worship, Tourist attractions

## 8. Methodology Notes

- All research conducted against `goldcoast.qld.gov.au` and `cityplan.goldcoast.qld.gov.au` only.
- No URLs were guessed or assumed; URLs were discovered by scraping known authoritative pages and following confirmed links.
- The ePlan system at `cityplan.goldcoast.qld.gov.au/eplan/` is the definitive live version but is a JavaScript SPA and cannot be directly scraped. The OCOP PDF (Source 2) represents the last substantive update to the zone code and is consistent with what ePlan displays.
- The OCOP amendment PDF is in tracked-changes format (showing proposed vs original wording from City Plan Version 9). The adopted text (as at Version 10) reflects the new/inserted wording, which is what this document presents.
- For subdivision feasibility and development assessment, the Building height overlay map, Residential density overlay map, and Minimum lot size overlay map must be consulted via the ePlan system, as these are spatial data only available there.
