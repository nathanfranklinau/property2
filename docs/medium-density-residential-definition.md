# Medium Density Residential Zone — Gold Coast LGA Research

**Research Date:** January 2025  
**City Plan Version Reviewed:** Version 13 (commenced 9 December 2025)  
**Applicable Council:** City of Gold Coast, Queensland, Australia

---

## Summary

The **Medium Density Residential zone** in the Gold Coast Local Government Area is defined under the Gold Coast City Plan — a statutory planning instrument that commenced on 2 February 2016 and is currently at Version 13 (December 2025).

The zone is intended for medium-density apartment-style developments, typically multiple dwellings in an apartment format. It prioritises intensive urban consolidation, efficient use of infrastructure, and walkable, subtropical-character neighbourhoods. It is distinct from Gold Coast's newer **Low-medium density residential zone** (introduced in City Plan Version 10, July 2023), which targets dwelling houses and low-to-medium density housing typologies.

---

## Official Zone Purpose Statement

### Current Purpose Statement (City Plan Version 10 onwards — as at Version 13)

The purpose statement below was formally adopted as part of the **Our City Our Plan Major Update 2 & 3** amendment, which commenced as **City Plan Version 10 on 4 July 2023**. It has not been altered in subsequent amendments (Versions 11, 12, or 13 were all minor and administrative amendments only).

> **The purpose of the Medium density residential zone code is to provide for:**
>
> **(a)** a range of medium density residential activities, generally in an apartment format, supported by community uses and small-scale services that serve local residents' daily needs;
>
> **(b)** intensive urban consolidation to maximise the efficient use of physical and social infrastructure; and
>
> **(c)** well-designed, walkable neighbourhoods with buildings that reinforce a distinctive subtropical character and positively respond to its urban context.

**Source:** Appendix 4 — Clarification Paper: Zone Purpose Statements (December 2021), Appendix A: Zone code purpose statement comparison (Medium density residential row — proposed/amended column). This proposed purpose statement was approved by the Minister under Section 6(3) of the *Planning Regulation 2017* and adopted in City Plan Version 10 (4 July 2023).

## Historical Context

### Pre-Version 10 Purpose Statement (City Plan Version 8, in force until 3 July 2023)

Before the Our City Our Plan amendment, the purpose statement for the Medium Density Residential zone was:

> The purpose of the medium density residential zone code is to provide for **multiple dwellings** supported by community uses and small-scale services and facilities that cater for local residents.

**Source:** Appendix 4, Appendix A — "City Plan Version 8" column for the Medium density residential row.

## Queensland Planning Regulation 2017 — State Standard

The *Queensland Planning Regulation 2017* sets a default (standardised) purpose statement for Medium Density Residential zones that applies across Queensland. Gold Coast's adopted purpose statement is a **modified version** approved under Section 6(3) of that Regulation, which permits the Minister to approve alternative purpose statements where they are considered necessary or desirable having regard to the circumstances of the local government area.

The Queensland state standard purpose statement is:

> The purpose of the medium density residential zone is to provide for—
>
> **(a)** medium density multiple dwellings; and
>
> **(b)** community uses, and small-scale services, facilities and infrastructure, to support local residents.

**Source:** Appendix 4, Appendix A — "Planning Regulation" column for the Medium density residential row.

---

## Why Gold Coast Adopted a Modified Purpose Statement

Gold Coast submitted a detailed justification to the Minister (Appendix 4, December 2021) for why its version of the purpose statement differs from the Queensland state standard. The key reasons stated were:

1. **Built form and urban design outcomes:** Gold Coast's 'Built Form and Urban Design Outcomes in City Plan' project found that urban and architectural design requirements could be strengthened in the City Plan's zone codes. The revised purpose statements were intended to "better cover intended built form, urban design and character outcomes sought by the City in these zones."

   > *"The current Purpose statements contained in City Plan and the Regulation are mainly land use focused and do not cover the intended character or nature of development."* — Appendix 4, Section 3.1

2. **Coastal development intensity:** Gold Coast noted unique local circumstances that warranted the modified approach:

   > *"The City considers that development within the abovementioned zones on the Gold Coast is unique to this local government area, with increased development intensity pressure in a distinctly coastal environment."* — Appendix 4, Section 3.2

3. **State interest integration:** The modified purpose statements integrate state interest requirements (e.g. well-designed, safe and accessible environments, infrastructure efficiency).

4. **Public consultation:** The modified purpose statements had already formed part of the first round of public consultation and formed a basis for community input. Reverting to the state standard would potentially "undermine the statutory consultation process."

5. **Legislative basis:** The amendment was commenced under former SPA legislation. At the time of State interest review and public consultation, the purpose statements were not required to align with the Queensland Planning Regulation 2017 standardised requirements.

**Source:** Appendix 4, Sections 3.1, 3.2, 3.3, 3.4 and Conclusion (Section 4).

## Key Characteristics of the Zone (Interpretation)

Based on the purpose statement and amendment documentation, Medium Density Residential zoned land in the Gold Coast is intended for:

- **Multiple dwellings**, principally in **apartment format** (as distinct from townhouses/duplexes, which are more characteristic of the Low-medium density zone)
- **Intensive urban consolidation** — maximising use of existing physical and social infrastructure such as transport, utilities,, parks, and services
- Development that **positively responds to the subtropical Gold Coast context** through built form and urban design
- **Walkable neighbourhoods** where buildings are well-designed and reinforce a distinctive local character
- Local-scale **community uses and small-scale services** to support residents' day-to-day needs

---

## Introduction of the Low-Medium Density Residential Zone

As part of the same Our City Our Plan Major Update 2 & 3 amendment (Version 10, July 2023), Gold Coast introduced a new **Low-medium density residential zone** as a separate zone between Low density residential and Medium density residential. This is relevant context because:

- Properties previously in certain medium-density transition areas may have been rezoned to the new Low-medium density zone
- The Medium Density Residential zone is now understood to be distinctly apartment-oriented, with the Low-medium zone capturing housing diversity at a lower scale (dwelling houses through to medium-density multiple dwellings)

**Source:** Appendix 4, Appendix A — Low-medium density residential row.
